# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Field workers, operations analysts, and administrators who need to monitor, track, and manage household and incident data across Vietnam's geographic regions. Users are task-oriented, working in operational or analytical contexts where quick access to spatial data, weather conditions, and entity records drives decision-making.

## Product Purpose

A desktop dashboard application that visualizes Vietnam's geographic data through an interactive map, enabling users to track household and incident information, analyze province-level statistics, and integrate weather data. The app serves operational management needs by allowing users to manage records (create, view, update, delete) for households and incidents while providing analytics views for data-driven insights.

## Brand Personality

Functional, authoritative, clear. The interface prioritizes data legibility, task efficiency, and geographic context. It feels like a professional field tool — reliable and precise, not decorative.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-site aesthetics, decorative gradients, heavy illustrations, or narrative scroll patterns. Do not prioritize visual flair over data density or task speed. This is not a consumer-facing brand experience; it is an operational instrument.

## Design Principles

1. **Data first**: Information hierarchy serves the task. Primary data (map, key metrics, active records) dominates the layout; secondary actions recede.
2. **Geographic context always**: Map and spatial awareness should remain visible or easily accessible across screens, reinforcing the geo-centric nature of the product.
3. **Form efficiency**: CRUD flows for households and incidents should minimize steps and present fields in logical, scannable groups.
4. **Consistent component vocabulary**: Reusable widgets (province panels, stats tabs, weather cards) enforce coherence and reduce cognitive load.
5. **Accessible by default**: Use sufficient contrast, keyboard-navigable controls, and clear focus indicators since the user base includes diverse field conditions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA minimum. Ensure interactive map controls and list forms are keyboard-accessible. Respect reduced motion preferences. Use sufficient color contrast for data visualizations and status indicators, including patterns or labels that do not rely solely on color.
